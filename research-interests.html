My Research Interests

Overview

Currently, my research interests are broad. My first experience with research was in the lab of Dr. Nishanth Sunny in the Department of Animal and Avian Sciences at the University of Maryland, College Park. There, I analyzed the impacts of Branched Chain Amino Acids (BCAAs) on mitochondrial dysfunction, igniting a profound interest in research within me. Since then, I have broadened my research experiences to include stem cell biology and gene therapy technologies at Ibex Biosciences and seek to expand even further!

Metabolic Biochemistry

I was fortunate to have hands-on experience learning about intermediary metabolism through research as I was taking Biochemistry of Metabolism (BCHM 462) at UMD. The class itself was already very interesting to me, covering topics ranging from the mechanisms of the citric acid cycle to the pentose phosphate pathway, ketogenesis, glycolysis, gluconeogenesis, and polyketide synthases. However, with my firsthand experience in the lab of Dr. Sunny I was able to augment onto my established knowledge in class by creatively applying my understanding of metabolism to trace 13C labeled citric acid and ketogenic metabolites for our experimental model. With my existing understanding of metabolism and endocrinology, I worked alongside graduate students to interpret how isotopically labeled carbon intermediates were incorporated into the TCA cycle and the ketogenesis pathway through gas chromatography-mass spectrometry methods. The results of our findings have resulted in the publishing of several papers, of which I am a co-author of one. The paper (in the process of being published), titled "Branched Chain Amino Acids Alter Cellular Redox to Induce Lipid Oxidation and Reduce De Novo Lipogenesis in the Liver" discusses the impacts of a diet high in Branched Chain Amino Acids in the generation of Adipocytes and the breakdown of Lipids. My interest in metabolism and its endocrine regulators have directly resulted in me securing a position as a Guided Study Session (GSS) leader at UMD for the Biochemistry of Physiology (BCHM463). I look forward to teaching topics in metabolism and physiology to new students!

Gene and Stem Cell Therapy

While working as an undergraduate researcher at UMD, I reached out to local biotechnology companies in my area to experience research outside academia and in the industry. I ended up working at Ibex Biosciences. While working at Ibex Biosciences as a Research Associate, I became interested in a project which involved manipulating cell signaling pathways, specifically involved in the inflammatory response, to analyze competitive or synergistic roles of two cell signaling pathways in inflammation. Two other projects I became involved in at my time there involved improving stem cell therapy to become more clinically applicable through the exploration of transcription factors other than the Yamanaka factors to induce pluripotency (as the Yamanaka factors contain oncogenic or metastasis-inducing genes such as c-Myc and KLF4), and the induction of pluripotency from astrocytes to neuronal progenitor cells for the differentiation of astrocytes to neurons. Though everyone's research experiences may be different, I personally found that research in industry was much more relaxed than research in academia. Though there is a competitive edge in securing funding for new studies, there was not much motivation for companies to publish papers and make new discoveries. Instead, companies primarily sought to validate existing scientific discoveries and to license their products through patents.

Biophysics of Mechanosensitive Ion Channels

In the Fall Semester of 2023, I will be exploring a completely new area of research under Dr. Sergei Sukharev at the Institute for Physical Science and Technology (IPST) at UMD. Admittedly, venturing into this completely new field from my research background is daunting, but I hope to gain additional experience in academia that would prove valuable to me for pursuing a doctoral degree in the future. I'm open to experiencing a broad range of research such that I can declare with confidence what area I would like to focus on in pursuing my Ph.D.

I have read literature regarding Dr. Sukharev's research already, but I will update this page with more information as my research progresses!

Antibody-Conjugated Glycoengineering

In addition to the research at IPST, I will also receive training in LC-MS and HPLC methodologies as a Research Associate at GlycoT Therapeutics in the Fall Semester. Again, I believe in experiencing many fields of research so that I can be more confident with pursuing a Ph.D. topic. This industry research experience seems to be more heavily focused on chemistry than research I have been previously involved in. In particular, GlycoT's research entails chemoenzymatic synthesis of antibody-linked sugars for greater site-specific targeting capabilities of antibodies.

Endocrinology of Insulin-like Growth Factors (IGFs)

In the summer of 2023, I am going to the University of Tokyo in Japan to participate in a fully-funded, independent research project under the competitive AMGEN Scholars Program to conduct research in the molecular mechanisms of IGFs under various physiological conditions. Though the host lab (the lab of Dr. Shin-ichiro Takahashi) covers a vast range of topics, the project I am most interested in participating in (given my long history with an endocrine condition and my previous experience in metabolic research) are his projects on amino acid catabolism in relation to IGFs and the development of physiology and disease.
